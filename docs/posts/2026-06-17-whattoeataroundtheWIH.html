What to eat around the WIH

Posted on June 17, 2026


I just recently had surgery with Dr. Chet at the WIH (it went great!). One thing I noticed while preparing for my surgery was that I did not find anyone writing about what to eat near the hospital. While, the hospital does provide food for you, at the end of the day it is hospital food, it may be less bland that your traditional hospital fare in a western hospital, but it was often lacking. Some of the food choices were quite strange for my palette personally, starting the morning with three hard boiled eggs and soy sauce is definitely not my style.

Therefore I have collected the list of restaurants I went to, so you may have a rough idea about whether a place is worth going to. ## SukiHippo This was the first restaurant I went to, around two weeks after my surgery. SukiHippi is an all you can eat hotpot restaurant. There is a base price for the buffet, at 219 baht per person. This includes most of the soups except for the mala varieties (mala soups are an extra 99 baht surcharge). Most of the meat selections at this tier are pork, with only one choice of beef: “Crying Tiger”, which I believe refers to brisket. There is higher tier at 399 baht per person, which includes more selections of beef (brisket, tongue, and oyster blade – this is also known as flat iron steak.), and seafood. I think this place is definitely worth the visit, provided you feel comfortable walking up the two flights of stairs to cross the highway. I had the mala broths each time I visited, which are fairly authentic to southern style Chinese hot pot. The pricing is quite fair if you can eat, and the seats being mostly booths makes it easy to find a comfortable position post surgery. Overall, I highly recommend SukiHippo.

Side note: the first time we went to SukiHippo, they only charged us for one person, making the entire meal incredibly cheap. I think this was a mistake, unless they have a lunch time pricing deal which I am unaware about.
